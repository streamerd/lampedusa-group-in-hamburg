  • @Libya 2011
    With the beggining of the military invention of NATO against Libya many workers and foreigners had to escape from Libya. Because of the new situation for most of the workers it was not safe anymore to stay. There was a high risk that the army could force them to take guns and to get involved in the fight. The only option that time seemed to go back to the homeland, but it was difficult to move out from Libya. Another option was taking the risk to cross the sea.
  • @ Lampedusa, Italy 2011 & 2012
    Most of us have arrived at Italy via Lampedusa Island. But in Italy conditions were bad and there was no chance to find a job. That is why most of us decided to move from Italy to other European countries.
  • @Hamburg 2013
    We arrived to Hamburg as individuals.
  • Self determination April, 2013
    The starting of the political movement Lampedusa in Hamburg. We self-organised as a group looking for our right to stay and to work in Hamburg. Because Germany is part of the NATO it is also responsible for that we had to leave Libya.
  • "Occupy Rathaus" October, 2013
    At the beginning we decided to meet the Senat of Hamburg to find a group solution for us, but the Senat refused to meet us. So we decided to occupy the Hamburger Rathaus and in Octobre 2013 the Lampedusa group undertook their first occupation.
  • Great local help 2013
    We still didn’t have places to sleep, nor did we have permits to work. But St. Pauli Church and Caravan Hamburg helped us to find place in the church for 80 persons (“The Embassy of Hope”) and some of us could sleep at B5 International Collective Center and others at different places across Hamburg.
  • Networked solidarity 2013 - 2014
    The organisation “CARAVAN for the rights of refugees and migrants” stood with the Lampedusa group and we built together the political and mobilisation movement.
  • A year of activities 2014
    The beginning of the Lampedusa group to be part of Hamburg City history. We started as group to join the city struggle and fighting against racism, discrimination and violence against refugees, women and all kinds of violence against humanity.
    • Demonstration in Hamburg City with 25 thousands persons
    • Establishment of Lampedusa Football Club in Spring 2014
    • Protest at the Thalia Theatre against the mayor of Hamburg on March 19, 2014
    • New action: Act of civil disobedience in front of at Hamburg Rathaus in June 2014
    • Beginning of the campaign Lampedusa Professions in July 2014.
    • Lampedusa Emancipation Days in October 2014.
  • Our losses 2013 & 2014
    we regret the deaths of some members of the Lampedusa group.
  • Dear Hamburg Senate .. 2014
    We addressed our first open letter to the public stating that we refused to accept the dirty game from Hamburg Senate who wanted to trick the members of the Lampedusa group by offering “Duldung”.
  • Dear Green Party .. 2015
    Open letter to the Green Party Hamburg.
  • "Show must go on" 2015
    Formation of the Migration Theatre Club. When we started the theatre play that time in Hamburg we wanted look for a chance to us raise our voices and to reach the German citizens because the authority refused to accept our demands. And also we choose the art as voice for us to remove the racism and discrimination from our lives and to send our massage to the public because the media ignores us and our rights.
  • Conference @ Kampnagel 2016
    The group Lampedusa in Hamburg organised “The international conference of refugees and migrants” in Kampnagel in Hamburg City. The same year we also organized a protest against the SPD demand to remove the info tent.
  • Conference @ Kampnagel 2017
    Together with other groups we built “We ́ll come United Against Racism”
  • Our 5th anniversary June, 2018
    The Lampedusa group celebrated its anniversary: 5 years of resistance and struggle in Hamburg.
  • We are @ European Parliament October, 2018
    We assembled the Refugee and Migrants parliament in the European Parliament in Brussels.
  • Stop deportation! 2019
    Standing up against all kinds of rights wings to say: No to deportations of refugees!
  • Bring the tent back! March, 2020
    Hamburg authority removed the Lampedusa info tent because of corona virus. Also new struggle against some groups working underground to destroy the refugee movement is going on. Online press conference against the isolations of refugees and homeless was held.
  • Black Lives Matter! June, 2020
    We had the biggest manifestation at Rathausmarkt Hamburg against the killing of Blacks. Black Lives Matter!
